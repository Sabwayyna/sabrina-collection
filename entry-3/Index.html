About "Suspended Memoir"

This art installation is a deeply personal journey into the ever-changing nature of my dreams. I've transformed my dream experiences into a series of abstract drawings, with the aim of expressing the wide array of emotions and sensations I've encountered in the dreamworld.

My motivation for embarking on this unique artistic endeavor originates from my detailed dream journal. In the journal, I didn't just describe the visual aspects of my dreams; I also attempted to capture the profound and sometimes indescribable feelings that accompanied them. These emotions and shifting perceptions became the inspiration for this project.

The core of the installation consists of abstract representations of these recorded emotions and sensations. Each page within the installation acts as a window into my inner thoughts, offering a fusion of colors, shapes, and lines that encapsulate the ever-evolving emotional journey I experienced in my dreams. These abstract drawings serve as a testament to my ability to translate the inexpressible, visually conveying the ineffable, and giving form to the formless.

The entire experience is brought to life through digital projection, allowing me to immerse viewers in a world where dreams and reality merge. As I stand within this intimate and dreamlike setting, I'm transported to the intersection of dreams and reality, where the lines between sensation and imagination blur, illustrating the transformative and ever-changing nature of our dreams.

Materials:

// Digital paintings on acetate and tracing paper, fishing wire, image projection, bed.

Personal Note

  • For this page I wanted to show my first attempt on creating an art installation.
  • Creating my first art installation has been a transformative journey for me, intimately intertwined with the notion of change. I embarked on this deeply personal exploration of my dreams, aiming to capture the ever-shifting nature of my nocturnal adventures. Change, to me, to the essence of dreams is the constantly evolving and transitioning of a complex spectrum of emotions and sensations. In translating these ephemeral dreamscapes into a series of abstract designs, I sought to convey the profound transformations that occur within the dreamworld. Each stroke on the canvas mirrored the fluidity and unpredictability of my dream experiences, where emotions and sensations can shift dramatically.
  • Working with this new medium required a shift in my thinking. It wasn't about reproducing the exact visual details of my dreams; it was about conveying their emotions, sensations, and the intangible aspects. As I was in highschool (grade 10) at the time, this project showed me that art is a limitless avenue for self-expression, and stepping out of my comfort zone has been a valuable part of my artistic growth.
