A Look Through of my first Experimental Film

?

In the moral hierarchy, all living beings find a place. Humanity has established itself at the pinnacle.

A over-consumption of animal products and its horrendous production conditions within the industry is appalling, hence, we tend to look away from this heavy, inextricable problem. This leads me to investigate the relationship between our morality and animal life.

This film visualizes the horrors within the exploitation of animals, disguised by our normalization – anarchy in the order of humanity.

This film is inspired by my zine’s exploration (Entry 6) of horrors using distressing imagery. I want to visualize the zine’s fluidity within its continuous mark-making and abstraction through a film that creates an uncanny valley effect. The zine’s grayscale overlaying pages reminded me of a storyboard, leading me to challenge myself by expressing my visualization through filmography.

Walking with obscure shawdows

00:21

I wanted to showcase in a more literal manner on how human footprint are imprinted in this world. Using clipping masks, I showed a video of trash over the shadows of people walking. My overarching objective was to articulate the consequences stemming from our insatiable and, unrestrained consumption.

Eyes merged with a goat's eye

00:40

I aim to convey the profound connection and shared experiences between animals and humans through the literal fusion of our eyes with those of a goat. The juxtaposition of a monotone video featuring human eyes superimposed onto the gaze of a goat serves as a visual metaphor, underscoring humanity's prevailing dominance over the animal kingdom and the broader natural realm.

Fish gasping

00:37

My objective is to showcase the journey of many captive-bred animals, tracing the path that leads them from confinement to our dinner plates. In our attempts to disconnect from the animals we eat, we often suppress empathy for these creatures. While witnessing a fish gasping for air may evoke sadness, that sentiment often fails to translate when the same fish is lifeless on our plates.

Worms tangling

I aim for the imagery of these worms as a concept to encourage viewers to ponder their notions of both food and ethical eating. Placed at the lower rungs of the morality ladder, the shot is a deliberate reflection on the boundaries we establish in our perceptions of edibility and ethical consumption.

Abstract earth

This shot aims to symbolize an abstracted portrayal of Earth, delving into the idea that ultimately, animals and humans are interconnected synthetically on this planet.

*I do not advocate to not consume meat at all; rather, I encourage viewers to to see biases in their moral perspectives. The aim is to show reflection on the ethical nuances surrounding our choices, fostering a more mindful approach to the complex relationship between our dietary preferences and the lives of the animals involved.
