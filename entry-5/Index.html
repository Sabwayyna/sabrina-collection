Initial InspirationI

Discovering the brand Stussy, with its streetwear aesthetics, was a major inspiration through my design process. Their graphics, which effortlessly blended artistic expression with urban style, captivated me from the start of designing my brand. Stussy's influence has not only shaped my creative work but also my identity as a designer, reminding me that inspiration can come from unexpected places and that pushing boundaries is essential in the world of fashion.

ReferenceII

I used a picture of my mouth to begin my design. I wanted to express a feeling of pressure with the imagery of grinding teeth. The act of grinding one's teeth can be a physical manifestation of stress and pressure. I aim to illustrate an image of clenched or grinding teeth to symbolize the tension and strain in a literal way.

Process of DesignIII

Referencing the image of my teeth, I simplified the subject into more of a grayscale design. By first paint out into simplified flats, I then edited each layer to appear gltiched. I add more grain on top and scatter the grain around the design.

Final DesignIV

To create my final design, I duplicated the image above and inverted its layer to create the contrast. I then added the brand name on the top and bottom to create more of a composition for a logo. I wanted this design to be easily seen as a mouth, but distorted enough to see its artistical and hand-drawn elements.

"BITE"?V

Experiencing a significant change of environment, such as when my family made the life-altering decision to move to Canada, has always had a profound impact to how I am as a person. In the midst of this transformative journey, I turned to my artistic expression as a means to encapsulate this complex emotional experience. I used a picture of my mouth as the starting point for my design. I wanted to convey the feeling of pressure that accompanies such a monumental change. The imagery of grinding teeth, a physical manifestation of stress and pressure, became the centerpiece of my artwork. Grinding teeth, in this context, isn't just a representation of the physical act but a symbol of the emotional turmoil that I had during this period of change.
